## 【新题】The area you live in

### Q1: What are some changes in the area recently?

**A:** Recently, the neighborhood has seen significant improvements. New parks and bike lanes have been added, making it more eco-friendly. Additionally, several modern cafes and shops have opened, attracting younger crowds. These changes have transformed the area into a lively and vibrant community hub.

### Q2: Are the people in your neighborhood nice and friendly?

**A:** Absolutely, my neighbors are very warm and approachable. For example, they often organize community gatherings, which creates a strong sense of belonging. I especially appreciate how everyone looks out for one another, making the neighborhood feel safe and welcoming. This friendly atmosphere truly enhances the quality of life here.

### Q3: Do you live in a noisy or a quiet area?

**A:** I live in a relatively tranquil neighborhood, which allows me to focus on my studies without distractions from noise. The peaceful environment is perfect for unwinding after a busy day of lectures and assignments. It fosters a sense of calmness which is beneficial for my overall well-being and productivity. I truly appreciate the serenity that my quiet area provides.

### Q4: Do you know any of your neighbors?

**A:** Well, since I've only just moved into a new apartment, I am not familiar with any of my neighbors yet. Not only that, but I leave the apartment early in the morning and return late at night, so I don't really have chance to get to know them either.

### Q5: Do you like the area that you live in?

**A:** Of course, yes! There are so many aspects in this area that take my fancy. The climate of it is perfect for me, although the winter is a bit freezing. And the modernism there is what attracts me most. Living in this place, I feel that I can always keep up with the times.

### Q6: What changes have been made to the area recently?

**A:** Well, a lot of changes are taking place nowadays in my city. I can see more and more skyscrapers have been built and the public transportation system is becoming more diversified and completed. But for me, the most fantastic change is that there is greater diversity in restaurants! Now I can taste genuine foreign food in my city. How marvelous it is!

### Q7: Do you know any famous people in your area?

**A:** Actually yes! I am aware that a lot of movie stars come from where I live. I feel very proud every time I see them in a fantastic film. Also, there are some poets and writers who are living in the area that I come from. So, I suppose my city is an artistic and poetic area.

### Q8: Where do you like to go in that area?

**A:** The sports stadium next to the building I live in, is where I would like to go frequently. The stadium is not very spacious but well-equipped with plenty of sports facilities. Also, there is an indoor swimming pool with a small number of people so I can swim whenever I want. That's really awesome!

## 【新题】Work or study

### Q1: What subject(s) are you studying?

**A:** The main subjects I'm studying at the moment are financial management, micro-economics, statistics and audition. Apart from these major ones, I also have to go to classes like Chinese literature, politics and English. I only study these because they contribute to my GPA.

### Q2: What technology do you use when you study?

**A:** Well, I normally use my iPad for online lessons. You see, I've signed up for many online lectures because I like to use my spare time to increase my knowledge. I also use the Kindle for reading which helps me to learn more about various topics and broaden my horizons.

### Q3: Who helps you the most and how?

**A:** My mother is always there for me. Whenever I need help, she's the first one to provide it. She paid for my English training fees when I first entered college. It was a big sum of money at the time. That training course has helped me a lot since then.

### Q4: How much time do you spend on your studies each week?

**A:** I devote approximately 30 to 35 hours per week to my studies. This includes attending lectures, participating in tutorials, conducting research, completing assignments, and preparing for examinations. I believe it's crucial to allocate a significant amount of time to academic pursuits in order to excel in my chosen field of study. Additionally, I organize my schedule effectively to ensure a balance between my academic commitments and other aspects of life, such as personal interests, extracurricular activities, and socializing with friends.

### Q5: Do you prefer to study in the mornings or in the afternoons?

**A:** I wish I was a morning person because I'm actually more productive in the morning. However, it's so difficult for me to rise early in the morning, so I have to arrange most of my study plans in the afternoons. I'm so used to this study schedule now.

### Q6: Are you looking forward to working?

**A:** Yes, absolutely. I am working part time at the moment. I like to make my own money because it gives me freedom. I'm looking forward to my graduation when I can have a proper job, make a decent income and enjoy being able to manage my own money.

### Q7: Is it very interesting?

**A:** Learning another language is not only an interesting experience but also a challenging one. It's full of fun when it comes to American and British humor. With excellent English, you can watch English TV series and movies without subtitles. But on the other hand, it requires substantial amount of patience to remember a wide range of vocabulary and to use it in the appropriate situation.

### Q8: Do you like your subject? (Why?/Why not?)

**A:** Yes, of course I do. I would say I love the major I'm studying because learning another language is never simply about the grammar or the words themselves. It's more about the cultural background, serving as a way for communication and passing on the wisdom of humanity from generation to generation.

### Q9: Why did you choose to study that subject?

**A:** Well, finance wasn't my primary choice. To be honest, I was convinced by my parents that it was the most worthwhile subject to learn. Perhaps they were right. What I've learned has helped me a great deal in real life. I think I can think more rationally nowadays about any financial decisions I make.

### Q10: Do you think that your subject is popular in your country?

**A:** Yes, I believe that law is indeed a popular subject in my country. With the increasing demand for legal professionals and the significance of upholding justice, many students are drawn towards studying law. The evolving legal system and frequent high-profile cases have also contributed to its popularity and the desire to pursue a career in this field. In addition, the diverse career opportunities offered by the legal profession have further attracted individuals to take an interest in law.

### Q11: Do you have any plans for your studies in the next five years?

**A:** In the next five years, my plans for studies include expanding my knowledge in various fields, such as computer science and psychology. I aim to pursue higher education and engage in research projects to further understand these subjects. Additionally, I plan to participate in internships or exchange programs to gain practical experiences and broaden my international perspective. Overall, my goal is to become a well-rounded individual through dedicated studies and constant learning.

### Q12: Do you want to change your major?

**A:** No, I do not want to change my major. I have always been passionate about my field of study and I believe it aligns perfectly with my career goals. I enjoy the challenges and opportunities that my major offers, and I am excited to pursue further knowledge and skills in this area.

### Q13: What changes would you like to see in your school?

**A:** I would like to see an upgrade in the school's gymnasium. It would be great if we could have newer and more advanced sports equipment, as well as improved facilities for various sports activities. A renovated gym would encourage students to engage more in physical activities and promote a healthier lifestyle. It would also provide a better environment for both practice and competitions, fostering a sense of competitiveness and teamwork among students. Overall, an updated gym would enhance the overall sports culture in our school.

### Q14: What do you think is the most important at the moment?

**A:** At the moment, as a student, I believe the most important thing is to adapt to the changes brought by the Covid-19 pandemic. It is crucial to embrace online learning opportunities and develop self-discipline to stay motivated. Additionally, fostering mental well-being amidst uncertainties and maintaining a healthy balance between studies and personal life are also vital. Finally, staying informed about current events and being socially responsible in following safety protocols to help combat the spread of the virus is of utmost importance.

### Q15: What work do you do?

**A:** I'm working as a teacher at XXX high school/training organization right now. Usually I teach English lessons four days a week. Correcting my students' homework and other assignments is an essential part of my job. I also spend time taking care of and playing with the students outside the classroom.

### Q16: Do you miss being a student?

**A:** Yes and no. I miss the worry-free time as a student. Studying hard was my mission. I didn't have to worry about rent, office affairs, work productivity and so on. However, when I didn't have the freedom to make financial decisions, I had to discuss almost every financial matter with my parents. That's the part I don't miss.

### Q17: Do you want to change jobs?

**A:** I'm quite content with my current job and don't have any immediate plans to switch to another position. I find my work fulfilling and enjoy the challenges and opportunities it offers. Additionally, I have developed valuable skills and expertise in my field, which I believe will help me grow and advance in my career. Therefore, I'm committed to continuing my journey in my current role.

### Q18: What kinds of technology do you use at work?

**A:** In my current job, I use various technologies to efficiently carry out my tasks. These include software such as Microsoft Office for document creation and data analysis. Additionally, I utilize project management tools like Trello or Asana to organize and collaborate on team projects. Moreover, I use communication tools like Slack or Microsoft Teams to facilitate seamless communication with colleagues and clients. Furthermore, I'm familiar with industry-specific software and database management systems that are essential for my profession.

### Q19: Is your work interesting?

**A:** It's not particularly exciting, but it's very practical. You know, finance is related to almost every aspect of our life. Also, it's a hot major in the job market. With financial knowledge, I could at least find a decent job and live a reasonable life.

### Q20: Do you like your job?

**A:** I'm very fond of my job. Actually, teaching is more of a career instead of a job to me. As a child, I always dreamed of being a teacher, standing on the stage in front of the school. I love embracing my students every day and passing on the knowledge I have accumulated over the years. I've always had this feeling that as long as I stay with my students, I will always be energetic and passionate.

### Q21: Why did you choose to do that type of work (or, that job)?

**A:** There's no doubt that teachers play an important role in students' lives. Back in my school days, I was lucky to encounter many excellent teachers who've had a profound influence on me. Their passion for teaching and patience for caring inspired me to be a teacher myself. With this idea in mind, I chose to study English at university with the aim of becoming an English teacher.

### Q22: What are the benefits of being your age?

**A:** As a 25-year-old working professional, there are several benefits that come with my age. Firstly, I have the energy and enthusiasm to take up new challenges and work towards my career goals. Secondly, being in my mid-20s allows me to have a good balance between youthfulness and maturity, which helps me adapt to different social and professional settings. Additionally, at this age, I have gained some work experience and developed a certain level of expertise in my field, which gives me an edge in my job. Lastly, I have a strong network of friends and colleagues within my age group, providing a sense of support and camaraderie in both personal and professional life.

### Q23: What requirements did you need to meet to get current job?

**A:** To secure my current job, I had to fulfill several requirements. Firstly, having a relevant educational background was crucial. I needed at least a bachelor's degree in my field. Additionally, I had to possess prior experience and demonstrate strong skills in areas related to my job role. Fluency in English was a must, as it is the primary language used in my workplace. Moreover, I needed to excel in the interview process, showcasing my communication abilities, problem-solving skills, and adaptability. Meeting these qualifications significantly contributed to my successful attainment of my current job.

### Q24: Do you have any plan for your work in the next five years?

**A:** Oh, absolutely! In the next five years, I'm planning to thrive in my career by exploring new opportunities, acquiring additional skills, and taking on challenging projects. I aim to enhance my problem-solving abilities, develop leadership qualities, and build a strong professional network. Furthermore, I hope to contribute to my field through research and innovation, ultimately making a positive impact. Overall, I'm excited about the possibilities that lie ahead and can't wait to see where my hard work and determination take me!

## 【新题】Hometown

### Q1: Did you learn about the history of your hometown at school?

**A:** Actually, not really. In history class, the teacher only focused on textbooks. We were asked to memorize the historical events of the world and of our country, but nobody taught us about our hometown. Perhaps it's too small to be mentioned.

### Q2: What's your hometown famous for?

**A:** My hometown is renowned for its vibrant street food culture, particularly for the mouth-watering local delicacy called "Dragon's Breath". This popular snack consists of liquid-nitrogen-infused cereal puffs that emit a dramatic mist when eaten. It's a hit among both locals and tourists, adding a touch of whimsy to our culinary scene.

### Q3: How long have you lived there?

**A:** I have been a resident there for approximately five wonderful years now. It has been such a pleasure to call that place my home. The community is vibrant, and the surrounding nature is simply breathtaking, making everyday life serene. Living there has enriched my life in countless ways.

### Q4: Is that a big city or a small place?

**A:** That's actually a mid-sized city with a population of around half a million people. It's not too hectic like the big metropolises, but it's also not a tiny town where everyone knows each other. I find it has a nice balance between urban amenities and a slower pace of life, which suits me quite well.

### Q5: Do you think you will continue living there for a long time?

**A:** Good question! I imagine I will. You see, I spend most of the year living in the city, because my work is in the city. However, I have plans to go back home someday in the future. When I've made enough money to retire, I will move back to my hometown.

### Q6: Is there anything you dislike about it?

**A:** Well, if I have to name one, then it's the poor job prospects. Since it's only a small town, the government doesn't have any plans to invest in it. Compared to big cities, it has poor infrastructure and transportation facilities, so there aren't many promising jobs there.

### Q7: What do you like about your hometown?

**A:** Well, the most attractive thing about my hometown is that it has a number of scenic spots, like the West Lake, and Lingyin Temple. People of various age groups can enjoy or entertain themselves when they visit the West Lake. Retired people usually sing opera near that area.

### Q8: Do you like living there?

**A:** Of course. I made many friends while I was growing up. Most of them settled down in my hometown. I like to hang out with them. Also, my family all live in my hometown. If I live there, I can spend time with my parents too. Above all, I enjoy the great scenery back home.

### Q9: Do you like your hometown?

**A:** Definitely. I often tell my city friends about my hometown. The greenery, the nature, the sweet people, and the quiet evenings have captured me deep inside. I've traveled to many places, but I believe my hometown has the best to offer, at least to me.

### Q10: How long have you been living there?

**A:** I lived there until I turned 18. I left my hometown for university but in the past few years I have traveled back to my hometown at least twice a year to spend holidays. To me, it's always the best place to be.

### Q11: Please describe your hometown a little.

**A:** Well, it's a small town where the weather is mild. There's no major public transport facilities like a train station or airport, however, the roads are solid and lead to all directions. Traveling is easy if you have a vehicle. Most importantly, people there are very nice and modest.

### Q12: Is your hometown a big city or a small place?

**A:** My hometown is a small town. It has a small population so there's not even a train station, but it's quite a livable place. It isn't busy or crowded like a big city. People can manage their lives at a slow pace. Life is pretty enjoyable in a small town.

### Q13: Where would you like to live?

**A:** Ideally, I would be able to afford an apartment in the city that I'm working in now, and a town house in my hometown. I'll live in the city during the weekdays and travel back to my hometown to spend the weekends and holidays.

### Q14: Do you plan to continue living there?

**A:** I plan to buy a house in my hometown when I can afford it, and quite likely, I will travel back and forth between my hometown and the city that I'm living in now. That's because even though my hometown is beautiful, it doesn't have the kind of jobs that I am interested in.

### Q15: Where's your hometown?

**A:** I come from a small town in the middle of the country. It's an agricultural town so the scenery is pretty stunning with plenty of trees and bushes lining both sides of the streets. The buildings aren't very tall like in big cities, and people are quite nice, so it's a very lovely place.
